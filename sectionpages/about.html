About

Sam Roe is an investigative journalist who was part of the team of reporters at the Chicago Tribune that won the 2008 Pulitzer Prize for Investigative Reporting for an examination of hazardous toys and other children's products.

As a reporter, Roe also has been a Pulitzer Prize finalist four times: in 2000, 2011, 2013 and 2019. As an editor, he oversaw a Pulitzer finalist in 2022.

Roe's stories have sparked new federal laws, product recalls, congressional hearings, and health and safety reforms, including a U.S. ban on the export of mercury, a reduction in toxic chemicals in toys and household furniture, and major safety improvements at the nation's pharmacies. Articles on the hazards of the strategic metal beryllium prompted the U.S. government to pay $15 billion to injured nuclear weapons workers.

He was an investigative reporter for 14 years at the Toledo Blade and was on the Investigative Team for 19 years at the Chicago Tribune. He was also a regional investigative director for Gannett for four years, overseeing projects at the Milwaukee Journal Sentinel and 10 other Gannett properties in Wisconsin.

He teaches Investigative Reporting and Introduction to Journalism at Columbia College Chicago.
